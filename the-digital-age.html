1970 — 1979

Computing Leaves the Lab

A single chip began holding what once filled a room. The microprocessor turned computing from an institution into an idea anyone could wire together. In garages and basements, hobbyists started building machines no company had asked for.

1980 — 1989

A Computer for Every Desk

Floppy disks made software something you could hold, trade, and mail. The IBM PC and the Macintosh turned computing into a daily habit, and for the first time, the screen looked back at the person using it.

IBM PC · 1981 3.5" FLOPPY DISK · 1982 MACINTOSH · 1984

1990 — 1999

The World Goes Online

A modem's screech became the sound of arrival. The World Wide Web turned isolated machines into one shared place, and for the first time, distance stopped being the limit on who you could reach.

WORLD WIDE WEB · 1991 NETSCAPE NAVIGATOR · 1994 GOOGLE · 1998

2000 — 2009

The Internet Leaves the Desk

Broadband made waiting obsolete and search became instinct. Then a phone with no keyboard put the entire web in a pocket, and computing stopped having a fixed address.

BROADBAND · 2000 WIKIPEDIA · 2001 IPHONE · 2007

2010 — 2019

Everyone, Everywhere, Always On

Photos, files, and conversations moved off the hard drive and into the cloud. Social platforms turned billions of people into broadcasters, and the network itself became the computer.

2020 — Now

Machines Learn to Speak

Neural networks trained on the sum of written language began to write back. AI stopped being a tool you operated by hand and became one you could simply ask.
